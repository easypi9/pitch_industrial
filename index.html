Section 02

#2 - Problem

Industrial subcontracting is still managed like a pre-digital process.

Decision speed is constrained by: The result:

Industrial subcontracting is still managed like a pre-digital process.

The bottleneck isn’t manufacturing capacity — it’s coordination and trust across multiple parties.

Supplier discovery is unstructured and relationship-driven (finding the right capability for a specific scope still depends on “who you know”)

Offers arrive in inconsistent formats hard to compare (procurement teams spend time normalizing information instead of making decisions)

Supplier verification is fragmented (capabilities, compliance and documentation are scattered and repeatedly re-checked)

Execution + documentation are spread across disconnected tools (no single operational thread from RFQ to close-out)

Decision-making is slow and opaque on both sides.

Buyers wait days or weeks for technically validated offers.

Suppliers spend days internally assessing feasibility — checking material availability, machine capacity, process compatibility, workforce load, deadline feasibility (DL), and pricing assumptions across disconnected systems and teams.

Technical validation is manual and iterative.

Engineering, production planning, procurement, and management exchange multiple internal iterations before an offer can even be submitted.

Capacity visibility is unclear.

There is no real-time structured view of production windows, bottlenecks, or available manufacturing bandwidth.

Logistics, installation, service providers, and warehousing are coordinated separately , often only after award — creating downstream delays, cost overruns, and execution risk.

Decision speed is constrained by:

manual RFQ interpretation

spreadsheet-based cost calculations

repeated material and technology checks

internal approval loops

disconnected logistics coordination

As markets accelerate, slow coordination becomes structural disadvantage.

The result:

long cycle times

high internal labor cost per RFQ

rework and misalignment

delayed downstream logistics planning

risk exposure

and no compounding data advantage

Industrial subcontracting today is not limited by machines — it is limited by the speed and structure of decision-making.
